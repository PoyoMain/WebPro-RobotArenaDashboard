Lucky

Lucky is a heavyweight combat robot competing on the television series BattleBots. Built and operated by Team Lucky from Canada, the robot is known for its flipper-based weaponry, robust pneumatic system, and polished engineering inspired by the classic British-style launch flippers popularized in early robot combat.

Key facts

  • Type: Pneumatic flipper robot

  • Weight class: Heavyweight (250 lb / 113 kg)

  • Country of origin: Canada

  • Builder/operator: Team Lucky, led by Mark Demers and Rob Mace

  • Debut season: BattleBots Season 2 (2016 revival)

Design and mechanism

Lucky features a front-hinged aluminum wedge and a powerful CO₂ flipper capable of tossing opponents several feet into the air. Its design emphasizes durability, with a low-slung frame and heavily armored sides that allow it to deflect horizontal spinners. The pneumatic flipper system is built for quick recharge, enabling multiple flips within a single three-minute match.

Competition history

Lucky debuted in 2016 as a successor to Son of Whyachi team’s earlier design, Warhead-style influence evident in its engineering. Over multiple seasons, Lucky has faced high-profile opponents such as Hypershock, Stinger, and Cobalt. While its record has fluctuated, the robot remains a fan favorite for its classic pneumatic power and resilience against spinners.

Development and legacy

Team Lucky continuously refines the robot’s reliability—improving flipper valves, weight distribution, and armor. Later iterations added modular weapon mounts and more efficient gas systems. Lucky stands as one of the few modern BattleBots to maintain a traditional flipper aesthetic in an era dominated by vertical spinners, representing both technical craftsmanship and nostalgia for early robot-combat engineering.
